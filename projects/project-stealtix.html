A platform to compare and buy tickets at the best prices, Stealtix needed a website to reflect a strong presence in a highly competitive NYC market. A mini identity & social posts were also created to help set up the product for the future.
No disappointments.
Ticket pricing aggregators is a competitive market and when the city is NYC, thereâ€™s no room for error.
A thorough check of players currently in the market revealed that they were already dominant, meaning Stealtix would have to focus on being different to earn the trust of customers.
It also became apparent that the market was divided into players who were offering niche services but Stealtix wanted to offer tickets for every event possible.
One line that sums up the process
write that here you twat
Real-life meets digital.
Set up to offer tickets for every genre, Stealtix needed a design to showcase infinite choices.
This led to the homepage being an infinite scroll of events, categorised into niches and events that a potential customer would be interested in.
The idea was to relate with the reason for someone going out, rather that what the event was about.
Game of cards.
Events needed to be visual to pique interest, however no control over the event image meant losing details due to low contrast.
A gradient was added to make sure the text is always legible.
Options for badges were created to make it easier to add offers, discounts etc. on the go.
The stage is set
Humans are visual animals and being able to physically see your seat being booked is a huge push for clarity and delight.
Additional cards to show viewpoint of every seat along with prices lead to peace of mind and increase in trust from the customer.
Payment portals also push for transparency with the customer being prompted for details as the check out the the event venue and a breakdown of the total amount.
A common identity....
A mini-brand identity was created to make sure that Stealtix could expand into newer offerings.
Inspired by the idea of letting events do the talking, colours were kept minimal with a dominant black and an accent red, calling upon shades of grey to break up the monotone pattern.
The typeface Avenir had the best mixture of clarity and legibility, and was versatile enough to be used in almost any kind of situation.
....to go global.
Using the identity social media posts were created for Stealtix, paving the way for a future international release.
